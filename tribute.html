About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein, born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, was a theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. His famous equation, E=mcÂ², is recognized worldwide as the formula of mass-energy equivalence. Einstein received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, a pivotal step in the development of quantum theory.

Known for his curiosity and innovation, Einstein was not just a brilliant scientist but also an advocate for peace, freedom, and human rights. His contributions have left an indelible mark on science and humanity.

Key Achievements

  • Developed the theory of relativity (1905, 1915).
  • Nobel Prize in Physics for the photoelectric effect (1921).
  • Contributed to the development of quantum mechanics.
  • Published over 300 scientific papers.
  • A staunch advocate for civil rights and peace.
